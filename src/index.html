I have a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from Haverford College and a Master's degree in Computational and Mathematical Engineering from Stanford University. I worked for many years as a research engineer at SRI International before learning to develop interactive news applications at the Seattle Times and ultimately joining the San Francisco Chronicle's digital team.

I am an expert problem-solver and programmer, with years of experience with diverse datasets. I am passionate about visual storytelling and I find it incredibly rewarding to employ my research and programming skills to create tools for the public and to tell important stories.
